Using FTMO as the example, rules explained in plain numbers on a $10,000 account. Recommended firms listed below. — 2026-06-24
💡 KHAN SNIPER PRO is a TradingView indicator — it provides signals; execution is yours. Trading on a prop firm funded account (instead of your own small account) is the main way to trade large capital without risking your own money.

1. What is a prop firm / funded account?

A prop firm is a company that gives you capital to trade. The logic is simple:

  • You pay a small challenge fee (~$100–$150 for a $10K account) and take a Challenge.
  • If you hit the profit target (e.g. +8%) following their rules, you receive a Funded account.
  • You then trade their $10,000 (or $100K) and keep 80–95% of the profits. If you lose, it's not your money — only the challenge fee is at risk.
Example: Make +5% on a $10K funded account in a month = +$500 profit. With 80% split, $400 is yours. Your total capital at risk was only ~$100 in challenge fees.

3. The 2 rules that eliminate most traders

Over 90% of challenge failures are not from inability to profit — they come from breaching one of these two loss limits. Understand them well:

① Daily Loss — daily loss limit (5% = $500)

If you lose more than $500 in a single calendar day, you are immediately disqualified. This counts closed losses plus open (floating) losses combined. The limit resets at midnight UTC+2 (CE(S)T).

Daily loss line = Day's opening balance − $500
TimeActionDay P&LStatus
MorningBalance opens at $10,000$0✅
Trade 1−$150 loss−$150✅ safe
Trade 2−$200 loss−$350✅ (approaching $500)
Trade 3−$200 loss−$550❌ disqualified (exceeded 5%)
⚠️ Most common mistake: you are eliminated the moment floating loss hits −$500 on an open position — even if you haven't closed it. Stop your daily losses around $400 (leaves a 1% buffer).

② Max Loss — total loss limit (10% = $1,000)

If total equity drops $1,000 from the initial $10,000 — reaching $9,000 — you are fully disqualified. In 2-Step, this floor is static — even if your balance grows to $11,000, the floor stays at $9,000.

Hard floor (2-Step) = $9,000 (never changes)
Simply put: Daily Loss is your "one-day" limit; Max Loss is your "lifetime" limit. Both apply to equity (floating + closed).
